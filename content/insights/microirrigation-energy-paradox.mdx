More than half of California's irrigated farmland now runs on drip or microirrigation. The water savings are real — but on-farm electricity use has not fallen. In many cases it has *risen*. And as California's agricultural electricity rates continue to climb, energy has become one of the most significant and least-managed operating costs in irrigated agriculture.
